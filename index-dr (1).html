About Dr. Shapkin

I am a PhD-prepared Registered Nurse and Nurse Practitioner dedicated to advancing brain health and optimizing mental fitness and metabolic wellness.

I hold a Doctor of Philosophy in Nursing from the University of Victoria, a Post-Master Nurse Practitioner certification from the University of Alberta, and specialized credentials including GNC(c) and MSPC.

My work bridges clinical practice, research, and academia to advance brain health, resilience, and well-being. I'm committed to decolonization, health equity, and community-engaged leadership.

Clinical Practice

Evidence-Based, Person-Centered Care

My Approach

I believe mental fitness and metabolic wellness are the cornerstone of optimizing long-term health and well-being. My practice emphasizes self-awareness, positive thinking, stress management, and restorative sleep.

Populations Served

I support diverse populations in achieving balance, energy, and resilience throughout midlife and beyond, with specialized expertise in gerontology and menopause care.

Philosophy

My clinical work blends science with compassion, empowering individuals to understand and actively address the root causes of their health challenges.

Teaching Philosophy

Grounded in Social Constructivism and Learner-Centered Teaching

I view education as an active, relational process where knowledge is co-constructed through experience, dialogue, and reflection. My role as an educator is to create culturally safe learning environments that enable the advancement of student scholarship, professional growth, equity and inclusion.

I strive to cultivate curious, reflective, and relational practitioners who can think critically, engage compassionately, and contribute to advancing nursing practice and decolonization.

Research & Scholarship

Brain Health, Decision-Making, and Thriving Through Life Transitions

Research Focus

I am deeply curious about how health professionals make complex treatment decisions, and how those choices affect people and families living with memory loss and cognitive change.

My research reveals the tension between analytic reasoning and relational practices in clinical decision-making, emphasizing the importance of empathy, time, and relational trust.

Looking Ahead

My emerging research focuses on women's brain health, examining how midlife experiences of anxiety, cognitive change, and hormonal transition influence mental fitness, metabolic wellness, and overall vitality.
