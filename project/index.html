As a Nurse Practitioner, I have focused my clinical practice on **mental fitness and metabolic wellness**. I believe this is the cornerstone of my approach to helping people optimize their long-term health and well-being.

Drawing on my extensive experience in managing acute and complex chronic disease, I integrate my deep knowledge of brain health, gerontology, and menopause care into caring for people. In this role, I support diverse populations in achieving **balance, energy, and resilience** throughout midlife and beyond.

My Approach:

Grounded in evidence-based, person-centered care, I apply a **lifestyle management approach** that integrates metabolic health with mental fitness. My practice emphasizes:

  • Self-awareness and positive thinking
  • Stress management and restorative sleep
  • Building sustainable habits for enhanced energy, cognition, and physical well-being.

My clinical work blends **science with compassion**, empowering individuals to understand and actively address the root causes of their health challenges. I practice at **Evolvewell Integrative Medicine Center** where I collaborate with a multidisciplinary team to provide comprehensive care.

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the principles of **Social Constructivism** and **Learner-Centered Teaching**. I view education as an active, relational process where knowledge is co-constructed through experience, dialogue, and reflection.

I strive to create a learning environment that is open, curious, and culturally safe; a place that enables the advancement of student scholarship, professional growth, equity and inclusion.

Research and Scholarship ðŸ”¬

My research sits at the intersection of **brain health, decision-making, and thriving through life transitions**. I am deeply curious about how health professionals make complex treatment decisions, and how those choices affect people and families living with memory loss, cognitive change, while living with comorbid conditions.

My doctoral studies included a **systematic review** and a **qualitative study** showing how Nurse Practitioners navigate treatment decisions for older people living with both cancer and a neurocognitive disorder. This work revealed the tension between analytic reasoning and relational practices in clinical decision-making, emphasizing the importance of **empathy, time, and relational trust**.
